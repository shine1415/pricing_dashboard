Section 5

Age Anchor Analysis

How the optimal price is identified at each age — and how it translates across segments. Mirrors the methodology in the Age Anchor Analysis dashboard.

1. The anchor concept — why not just pick the highest CVR?

At each age, multiple price points have been observed with different CVRs and conversion volumes. Three signals compete:

SignalWhat it picksThe problem
Best CVRHighest conversion rateOften a price with 2–5 converters — noise, not signal
Most convertersHighest raw volumeCan be a low price that had more users exposed
Best balanced · anchor ✓Wins on both rate and volume—

The anchor formula is CVR% × √(conversion events). It rewards high conversion rate but scales it by evidence mass. Example at age 25: P25 had 20.7% CVR but only 34 converters (score 107). P26 had 14.5% CVR with 136 converters (score 182). P26 is the anchor — the lower rate is more than compensated by the evidence behind it.

2. iOS Dev — reference anchor

iOS Dev has the most conversion data (1,879 converters in the latest period). Its per-age anchor prices are the reference the cross-segment model uses to derive prices for all other segments. Prices rise by 1 tier per year — a clean linear relationship the data produces without assuming it.
