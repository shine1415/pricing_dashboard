How the ratios are computed: At each age where both iOS Dev and the target segment had ≥25 users + ≥2 converters, we took target best price ÷ iOS Dev best price and computed the median across all qualifying ages. For ages with direct A/B data, the cross-segment number is a consistency check — the recommendation comes from the direct A/B winner. Why iOS Dev is the baseline

iOS Dev has 1,886 total converters across ages 18–40 — the most of any segment. More data per age = more reliable anchor. Using a thinner segment would inject noise into all downstream estimates.

Why a higher price can win

In dating apps, price signals quality. Users sometimes convert because the price feels serious — not despite it. The engine always proposes both directions; the A/B result decides which effect dominates for that age and segment.

Period 1 winners → Period 2 single prices

Some segments (e.g. iOS Dev) ran an A/B test in Period 1 (Dec 19–Jan 29), then implemented the winning price as a single price in Period 2 (Jan 31–Mar 19). Those appear as "Live price · no A/B variant" — already validated, not untested.
